Carolina-Mecklenburg Schools (CMS)

Carolina-Mecklenburg Schools stands as one of the largest school districts in North Carolina and the 17th largest in the nation, serving over 140,000 students across 180+ schools. With a commitment to providing optimal learning environments while managing operational costs responsibly, CMS sought a partner to modernize their aging lighting infrastructure.

The Challenge

CMS faced several critical challenges with their existing lighting systems:


1. Rising Energy Costs

  • Outdated fluorescent and HID fixtures consuming excessive electricity
  • Annual lighting energy costs exceeding $500,000 across the 25 target schools
  • Budget pressures limiting resources for educational programs

2. Maintenance Burden

  • Frequent bulb replacements disrupting classroom activities
  • High labor costs for an understaffed maintenance crew
  • Safety concerns from ladder work in high-ceiling areas

3. Learning Environment Quality

  • Inconsistent lighting levels affecting student concentration
  • Flickering fluorescent tubes causing eye strain and headaches
  • Poor color rendering impacting visual learning materials
  • Inadequate lighting in gymnasiums, classrooms and common areas

4. Environmental Responsibility

  • District-wide sustainability goals requiring significant carbon footprint reduction
  • Community expectations for environmental stewardship
  • Alignment with state and local green initiatives

Carolina LED Pros developed a customized, phased approach to transform the lighting infrastructure across all schools while minimizing disruption to educational activities.

Technology Selection

LED Fixture Specifications:

  • Classroom Areas: 4000K color temperature LED retrofit strip kits with minimum 50,000 hour warranty
  • Gymnasiums: High-bay LED fixtures with 150+ lumens per watt efficiency
  • Exterior: Weather-resistant LED wall packs and parking lot fixtures
  • Emergency Lighting: Integrated battery backup LED exit signs and emergency lights

Performance Metrics

Before LED Conversion:

  • Average fixture wattage: 96-128 Watts
  • Average annual operating hours: 4,600
  • Annual energy use: 9.66 million kWh

After LED Conversion:

  • Average fixture wattage: 25W
  • Annual energy use: 6.16 million kWh

Energy Savings Performance

Annual Energy Reduction: 2,500,000 kWh

  • Equivalent to removing 385 cars from the road annually
  • CO2 reduction of 1,770 metric tons per year
  • 62% reduction in lighting energy consumption

Financial Benefits

Total Annual Savings: $1,000,000

  • Energy Cost Savings: $600,000/year
  • Maintenance Cost Savings: $75,000/year
  • Reduced HVAC costs: $55,000/year (LED heat reduction)

Duke Energy Rebates: $1,700,000

  • Immediate offset to project investment
  • Accelerated ROI timeline
  • Validation of energy efficiency measures

Educational Environment Improvements

Student and Teacher Benefits:

  • 35% improvement in average light levels
  • Elimination of flickering and buzzing
  • Reduced eye strain and fatigue
  • Improved color rendering for visual learning
  • Enhanced safety in parking lots and walkways
