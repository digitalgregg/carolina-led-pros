Martin Sprocket & Gear

Martin Sprocket & Gear stands as a leading manufacturer of power transmission and material handling products, serving industrial markets across North America. Their Carolina facility operates as a critical distribution and manufacturing hub, running multiple shifts to meet customer demands. With rising energy costs impacting operational margins and aging lighting infrastructure requiring constant maintenance, Martin Sprocket & Gear sought a partner to modernize their facility lighting while maintaining uninterrupted production schedules.

The Challenge

Martin Sprocket & Gear faced several operational and financial challenges with their existing lighting system:

1. Excessive Energy Costs

  • Outdated metal halide high-bays in warehouse consuming 400W per fixture
  • T12 fluorescent fixtures in offices nearing end of life
  • Exterior HPS fixtures running all night at high wattage
  • Annual lighting costs exceeding budget allocations

2. Maintenance Burden

  • Frequent high-bay lamp failures requiring lift rental
  • Production disruption for warehouse lighting repairs
  • Safety concerns when changing fixtures at 30+ foot heights
  • Stockpiling expensive replacement bulbs

3. Inadequate Light Quality

  • Dim conditions in picking/packing areas affecting accuracy
  • Long warm-up times for metal halide fixtures after power events
  • Poor color rendering making part identification difficult
  • Inconsistent lighting levels across work zones

4. Safety and Compliance

  • Below-OSHA recommended foot-candles in work areas
  • Dark spots in exterior loading areas creating hazards
  • Emergency lighting reliability concerns
  • Insurance recommendations for improved facility lighting

Carolina LED Pros developed a strategic approach to transform Martin Sprocket & Gear's lighting infrastructure while maximizing available utility incentives and minimizing operational disruption.

Technology Deployed

Warehouse Areas (125 fixtures)

  • High-Bay LED Fixtures: 150W LED replacing 400W metal halide
  • Features: 20,000+ lumens, 5000K color temperature, instant-on capability
  • Controls: Motion sensors in low-traffic aisles for additional savings

Office Spaces (42 fixtures)

  • LED Panel Troffers: 40W LED panels replacing 128W T12 fluorescents
  • Features: 4000K color temperature, 90+ CRI, dimmable capability
  • Benefits: Reduced eye strain, improved worker comfort

Exterior Areas (20 fixtures)

  • LED Wall Packs & Flood Lights: 80W LED replacing 250W HPS
  • Features: Photocell and timer controls, dark sky compliant
  • Security: Enhanced visibility for loading docks and parking areas

Smart Controls Integration

  • Occupancy sensors in warehouse aisles (30% additional savings)
  • Daylight harvesting near dock doors and skylights
  • Programmable scheduling for office and exterior zones
  • Emergency lighting test features built into fixtures
